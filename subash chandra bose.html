Subash Chandra Bose


Subhas Chandra Bose (23 January 1897 – 18 August 194) was an Indian nationalist
whose defiance of British authority in India made him a hero among Indians,
but his wartime alliances with Nazi Germany and Imperial Japan left a legacy vexed by authoritarianism, and military failure.
, The honorific Netaji (Hindustani: "Respected Leader") was first applied to Bose in Germany in early 1942—by Indian soldiers of the Indische Legion and by the German and Indian officials in the Bureau for India in Berlin.
It is now used throughout India.[u]
